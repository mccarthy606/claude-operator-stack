# Founder DMs — launch day primer outreach

Five short DMs to send within the first four hours of going public. Each is for a different relationship shape. Read before sending — personalize the [bracket] tokens.

The point of these DMs is *not* to ask for a star or a retweet. It is to give a heads-up to people whose opinion matters to you, on the principle that the people closest to your work hear about it from you, not from an algorithm. If a star or share comes from one of these, that is a side effect, not the ask.

All five fit in 500 characters or fewer so they read as a real DM, not a press kit. None of them open with "hey".

---

## DM 1 — to a fellow solo founder you've talked to about Claude Code

For the operator-peer who has DM'd you about workflow questions before. Same archetype as you. Will recognize the shape immediately.

> [name] — finally pushed the stack public this morning: github.com/mccarthy606/claude-operator-stack. The thing we kept circling on in DMs (how I keep N projects in flight without the context-switching tax) is in the workflows/ and profiles/ directories. Honest read welcome — I trust your eye more than the timeline's. No share needed. Will buy the coffee next time.

(366 chars.)

## DM 2 — to a YC / AngelList / investor connection (more formal)

For someone who knows you in a professional context. They are not the audience for the product but they care about your ability to ship. Keep it tight, lead with the metric, no familiarity tax.

> [Name] — quick heads-up. Open-sourced the stack and playbook I have been running for the last four months: github.com/mccarthy606/claude-operator-stack. Seven products built on it, MIT-licensed, written for the operator audience. Not pitching anything, just thought you would want to see what came out of the work since we last spoke. Hope you are well.

(353 chars.)

## DM 3 — to a content creator in your niche

For the YouTuber, podcaster, or newsletter writer who covers AI tooling or solo-founder workflow. They may or may not cover this, and that is fine — the DM is a relationship touch, not a press pitch.

> [name] — just shipped this and thought it might interest you given [their last video / newsletter on X]: github.com/mccarthy606/claude-operator-stack. It is the install path and playbook for running multiple AI products solo with Claude Code, with a cookbook of twelve real recipes and four operator profiles. Not asking for a feature; if you ever want to dig in, I am happy to walk you through any piece.

## DM 4 — to someone who has open-sourced their own Claude / AI tooling (peer maintainer)

For another maintainer in this corner of the ecosystem. Possibly someone whose work is *credited in* your repo. Acknowledge that explicitly — it is the right thing to do, and it pre-empts any "who is this person" reaction.

> [name] — credited your work in the stack I just open-sourced: github.com/mccarthy606/claude-operator-stack ([component] is in the credits and linked from stack/[file].md). Wanted you to see it before the timeline did. If anything in how I framed your project lands wrong, tell me directly and I will fix it the same day. Thanks for the original work — building on it has been the easy part.

(390 chars.)

## DM 5 — to a non-technical founder friend

For the friend who runs a non-AI business but is curious about how you have been spending your time. They are not the audience for the install path but they will appreciate the human update. Keep it warm, no jargon.

> [name] — pushed the thing I have been working on public today: github.com/mccarthy606/claude-operator-stack. It is the toolkit and playbook I have been using to run several AI products at once, written for people without a CS background. Not relevant to your business directly, but if you are ever curious how this stuff is actually run day to day, the case studies are the readable part. Hope all is good with [their company / kid / city].

(440 chars.)

## Notes for the operator

- **Send to 5-10 people total, not 50.** Quality over volume on launch day. A bulk-blast read as a bulk-blast and devalues every other DM you have sent that person.
- **Personalize every [bracket] token.** No exceptions. If you can't think of a personal hook in 30 seconds, that person is not on the list today.
- **Send one at a time, with 5-10 minute gaps.** That is so that if a reply comes in you can answer in real time. Do not batch-fire all five and then disappear.
- **Do not ask for a star, share, or retweet explicitly.** The implicit ask in "wanted you to see it" is sufficient. People who care will share; people who don't shouldn't be guilted into it.
- **No follow-up if there is no reply.** A non-reply is information. Do not bump in 48h.
- **Keep a list of who you sent to + when.** Not for tracking conversion — for not double-DMing in three months when v1.1 ships.
- **The DMs go after the X thread is up but before the Show HN submission.** Order: thread → DMs to people who are on X → Show HN. That way the people you DM can see the thread already exists if they want to react publicly.
- **If a recipient asks "how can I help" — have an honest answer ready.** Best three: (1) honest read, tell me what is unclear; (2) if you know a solo founder who would use this, send it to them; (3) star is fine but the read matters more.
